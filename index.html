Trading fundamentals

No fluff. Five essential topics every trader should understand before putting real money at risk.

The stock market is a marketplace where buyers and sellers exchange shares of publicly traded companies. Prices move based on supply and demand, driven by earnings, economic data, news, and sentiment.

BULL MARKET
Prices trending higher over time. Optimism drives buying.
BEAR MARKET
Prices trending lower. Fear and uncertainty drive selling.
MARKET ORDER
Executes immediately at the current market price.
LIMIT ORDER
Executes only at your specified price or better.

💡 Start with paper trading (simulated trades, no real money) to build confidence before risking capital.

MA
Moving Average — Smooths price data to reveal trend direction. The 20, 50, and 200-day MAs are the most watched. Price above MA = bullish bias. Price below = bearish.
RSI
Relative Strength Index — Measures momentum on a 0–100 scale. Above 70 = potentially overbought. Below 30 = potentially oversold. Most useful in ranging markets.
MACD
Moving Avg Convergence Divergence — Shows relationship between two EMAs. A bullish signal occurs when the MACD line crosses above the signal line.
VOL
Volume — Number of shares traded. High volume confirms a price move. Low volume moves are unreliable and often reverse.
VWAP
Volume Weighted Avg Price — The average price weighted by volume throughout the day. Institutions use it as a benchmark. Price reclaiming VWAP is a bullish intraday signal.

Options are contracts that give you the right — but not the obligation — to buy or sell a stock at a specific price before a set expiration date.

CALL OPTION ↑
The right to BUY shares at the strike price. Profitable when the stock goes up.
PUT OPTION ↓
The right to SELL shares at the strike price. Profitable when the stock goes down.
Strike PriceThe price at which you can buy/sell the underlying stock.
ExpirationThe date the contract expires. Options lose value daily as expiry approaches (theta decay).
PremiumThe price you pay to buy the options contract.
ITM / OTMIn the money = has intrinsic value. Out of the money = higher risk, higher reward potential.

⚠️ Options expire worthless if the trade doesn't work. Never risk money on options you don't fully understand.

Your mindset is your most valuable trading asset. Studies show that most losses come from emotional decisions — not bad analysis.

😰
FOMO — Chasing a move after it's already happened. You buy the top, the stock reverses, you sell the bottom. The answer: if you missed it, let it go. The next setup is always coming.
😤
Revenge Trading — Taking impulsive trades after a loss to "win it back." Almost always leads to larger losses. After a big loss, walk away. The market will be there tomorrow.
🤑
Overconfidence — A winning streak leads traders to size up too large and skip their process. One bad trade can erase weeks of gains. Stay humble, trust your plan.

📓 Keep a trade journal. Write down why you took every trade. Your patterns — both good and bad — will become obvious within weeks.

Protecting your capital is more important than making money. A trader who loses 50% of their account needs a 100% gain just to break even.

1% RULE
Never risk more than 1–2% of your total account on a single trade.
2:1 R/R MINIMUM
Only take trades where your target is at least 2× your stop loss distance.
STOP LOSSES
Set them before you enter. Move them only in your favor, never against.
DAILY LIMIT
If you lose 3% in a day, stop trading. Walk away and reset tomorrow.

📐 With a 2:1 R/R ratio, you only need to be right 40% of the time to be consistently profitable. Focus on quality setups, not trade frequency.

Level Interpretation

How To Use Trade Map

Understanding how to interpret QQQ projected high and low levels in your trading day.

Trading Above Expected High

When QQQ is trading above the projected high, it signals underlying strength. Price tends to continue higher as buyers remain in control and supply is absorbed. This environment favors momentum and breakout setups to the upside.

Trading Below Expected Low

When QQQ is trading below the projected low, it signals underlying weakness. Price tends to continue lower as sellers are in control and demand is overwhelmed. This environment favors momentum and breakdown setups to the downside.

Bounce at Expected Low — Long Opportunity

When QQQ trades down to the projected low and shows signs of holding, it can create an intraday long opportunity. Price reacting to the level with reduced selling pressure or a visible reversal pattern is a signal that the low may hold for the session.

Rejection at Expected High — Short Opportunity

When QQQ reaches the projected high and fails to push through, it can create an intraday short opportunity. Watch for stalling price action, exhaustion, or a rejection candle at the level as confirmation that buyers are losing momentum.
